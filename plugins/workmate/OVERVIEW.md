# Meet Workmate

**A conversational AI layer for Workamajig.**

Workamajig is the system of record for your agency — projects, time, billing, AR, AP, pipeline, P&L, all of it. Workmate adds a new way to work with that data: natural language. Ask a question, get an answer. Describe a meeting, log the activity. Say what you need, and Workmate drafts it against the right project, the right account, the right approval routing.

> "Give me the cash picture."
> "What did we last talk to Acme about?"
> "Log the call I just finished with Sarah — she wants the SOW updated by Friday."
> "Who's available for a 40-hour pitch next week?"

Everything still lives in Workamajig. Workmate just gives you a new way to reach it.

---

## What Workmate adds

Three new capabilities, layered on top of the Workamajig data you already have:

**Conversational analysis.** Briefs, triage lists, and synthesized reads — generated on demand from your live data. AR aging rolled up to client-level severity. Pipeline scored on five factors. Project health flagged with PM and AM named. Whatever you'd ask your controller, ask Workmate.

**Natural-language capture.** Log a meeting by describing it. Log time by listing what you worked on. File an expense from a receipt photo. Create a task by saying what needs to happen. Every capture is shown as a draft, you confirm, then it lands in Workamajig with the right fields filled.

**Multi-step workflows.** Some things naturally chain: closing a deal triggers project creation, kickoff activity, initial task list. Closing out your week means checking time, expenses, and approvals. Workmate composes the steps and walks you through them with a single confirmation.

## What you can ask it to do

### Tell you where the money is

> *"What's our cash picture?"*
> Current bank balances, week-over-week change, projected inflows and outflows for the next 30/60/90 days, where the cliffs are.

> *"Who owes us money worth chasing?"*
> AR aging rolled up to the client, with the worst offenders surfaced and chase-note drafts ready to copy into email.

> *"Can we make payroll Friday at $250K?"*
> The math: cash today, expected inflows by Friday, outflows in between, and the worst-case ending balance. Plus a short list of AR you could push this week to close any gap.

> *"What's our margin on Brand Workshop work?"*
> Revenue by service item, ranked by volume and margin proxy, with the under-rated items called out.

### Show you the agency's health

> *"How's the agency?"*
> Cash, AR, AP, pipeline, project health, your approval queue — one screen, balanced view.

> *"Are we too dependent on one client?"*
> Top-10 clients with their revenue share, HHI concentration index, and movers since last period.

> *"Which projects need a conversation?"*
> Over-budget projects with PM and AM named, near-budget projects to watch, behind-schedule projects with days late.

> *"Quarterly review for Q1."*
> Board-ready brief: P&L vs. prior quarter, revenue mix, client mix, closing positions, three things that worked and three that didn't.

### Triage your pipeline

> *"Who should I call this week?"*
> Top 5–10 leads ranked by composite score, with a one-line opener drafted for each based on stage, value, and recency.

> *"Score my pipeline."*
> Every open lead, scored 0–100 on stage progress, recency, value, time-to-close, and owner engagement. Hot, warm, and cold tiers.

> *"Clean up the CRM."*
> Stale leads, stalled-in-proposal leads, orphans without an owner, leads past their estimated close. Bulk recommendations.

> *"Sales are flat. What kind of campaign would help?"*
> Pipeline diagnosis (top-of-funnel gap vs. close gap), look-alike target segment based on recent wins, suggested angle.

### Capture work as you go

> *"I just finished a call with Sarah at Acme. We agreed on the production timeline, Mike will revise the SOW by Wednesday, and they want a status update on Friday."*
> Draft activity log, two follow-up tasks (Mike's SOW, Friday status), confirm, done.

> *"Log 3 hours on Acme strategy this morning and 1.5 on the Globex pitch."*
> Matched to your assignments, drafted, confirmed, posted.

> *"File this receipt"* (attach photo)
> Values pulled from the image, project resolved from context, billable cost computed with the right markup, attached to your open envelope.

> *"Add a task — Mike needs to deliver the brand brief by Thursday."*
> Right project, right owner, right due date — a task created against the right project in ten seconds.

### Prepare you for things

> *"Prep me for my 9:30."*
> Pre-meeting brief: who's coming, what their account looks like, the project they care about most, the recent thread, talking points, anything not to surprise them with.

> *"Tell me about Initech."*
> Client deep-dive: open AR, trailing 12-month revenue, what you bill them for, active projects, open pipeline, recent activity, an honest read on the relationship.

> *"Who's available next month?"*
> Resource picture by person: available, tight, slammed, over-committed. Honest about whether you can staff that pitch.

### Run multi-step workflows

> *"We just closed the Acme brand refresh."*
> Create the project shell, mark the opportunity closed-won, seed the initial task list, log the kickoff, queue up your AM to-dos — one confirmation, all of it.

> *"Close out my week."*
> Time check (any gaps?), expense envelope (ready to submit?), approval queue (clear them now?) — Friday wrap in five minutes.

> *"Scope creep on ACME-Q4-CAMPAIGN. We need a change order."*
> The math (budget vs. cost vs. billed), the gap, a draft narrative citing the conversations where the scope expanded, a suggested dollar amount with a small buffer.

**The CFO / controller.** Month-end close, P&L vs. prior, transaction register review, 1099 prep, AR/AP at quarter-end — synthesized into briefs ready to send to the accountant.

**The new-biz lead.** Lead triage, call lists, opportunity recaps. Campaign planning when pipeline thins.

**Everyone.** Time logging that feels like a conversation. Expenses captured from a photo. Tasks created by saying what you need.

## How it works with Workamajig

**Same data, same permissions, same approval chains.** Workmate reads through the Workamajig MCP server, which uses the same stored procedures and security model as the Workamajig UI. Your GL company access, project assignments, and rights (acct_ar_aging, acct_gl_pnl, approval rights, etc.) all carry over exactly. If you can't see it in Workamajig, Workmate can't show it to you.

**Read by default. Writes confirmed.** Analysis skills (briefs, deep-dives, triage) are pure read. Capture skills (logging meetings, time, expenses, tasks, vouchers) follow a strict "draft → confirm → act" pattern: nothing is written to Workamajig without you saying yes on a shown draft.

**Multi-company aware.** If your access is scoped to one GL company, everything Workmate shows is scoped the same way. Multi-company agencies can tell Workmate which company to focus on day-to-day.

**Workamajig stays the source of truth.** Records created via Workmate live in Workamajig like any other record — same audit trail, same triggers, same downstream integrations. Workmate is a way *into* Workamajig, not a parallel database.

1. Pick a starting area — cash, pipeline, projects, month-end. Claude runs the matching analysis right then against your live data.
2. Quick interview — agency type, headcount, top three focus areas, GL company scope, capture preferences.

After that, just ask. Say what you need in your own words. Workmate routes to the right skill, shows you the right data, drafts the right action.

## A short list of what Workmate won't do

- **Send things on your behalf.** Drafts only. You're the one who hits send.
- **Move money.** No payments, no journal entries, no invoice creation. Those stay in Workamajig with their proper approval routing.
- **Replace your team.** Surface, summarize, suggest. Decisions stay with you.
- **Pretend to know things it doesn't.** If a field is empty in Workamajig, Workmate says so. No invented context.
- **Train on your data.** Workmate reads to answer; it doesn't learn from your tenant.
